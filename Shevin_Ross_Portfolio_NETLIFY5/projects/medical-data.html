Key Findings & Insights

Summary of analytical conclusions drawn from the data

Finding 1: Cardiac and respiratory conditions account for 38% of all admissions but 61% of total treatment costs, indicating a significant cost burden from chronic disease management.
Finding 2: Average length of stay declined from 5.5 days in 2019 to 4.7 days in 2024, likely driven by improvements in surgical techniques and early discharge protocols.
Finding 3: Emergency admissions peaked in 2020–2021 (COVID-19 impact), then declined steadily as elective procedures resumed post-pandemic.
Recommendation: Allocating additional resources to preventive care programs could reduce cardiac admissions by an estimated 15–20%, generating significant cost savings.
