Key Findings

Insights from exploratory analysis of global pandemic data

Finding 1: Countries that achieved 70%+ vaccination rates before the Omicron wave saw 58% lower hospitalisation rates compared to those below 40% coverage, strongly validating vaccine effectiveness.
Finding 2: The Delta wave (mid-2021) represented the deadliest surge globally, with a 340% spike in daily deaths compared to baseline, despite growing vaccine availability.
Finding 3: Sub-Saharan Africa recorded significantly lower case fatality rates (0.7%) than Europe (1.6%), likely influenced by younger population demographics and possible underreporting.
Recommendation: Pandemic preparedness strategies should prioritize rapid vaccine distribution infrastructure, particularly in low-income nations, to minimize mortality in future outbreaks.
